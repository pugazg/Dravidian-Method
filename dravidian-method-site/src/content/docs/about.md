The Dravidian Method is a long-form digital book about a recurring political and institutional question in Tamil Nadu.

It does not treat the Dravidian tradition as a fixed catalogue of policies. It reads the tradition as a repeated act of diagnosis: each generation asks what has become concentrated, then searches for ways to widen participation again.

The book follows that pattern through dignity, ownership, opportunity, and knowledge. Its conclusions are intentionally provisional because the newest chapter, ownership in an economy organised around research, intellectual property, venture capital, and innovation ecosystems, is still unfolding.

## Central Question

> What happens when too much power over human destiny becomes concentrated in too few hands?

## Editorial Position

This is not an official history, a manifesto, or a catalogue of government schemes. It is an interpretive essay grounded in publicly verifiable sources, historical texts, policy documents, and institutional evidence.
