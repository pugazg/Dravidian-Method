The Dravidian Method is an interpretive essay, not an official history.

It is not a manifesto, a campaign document, or a catalogue of achievements. It follows a recurring question across dignity, ownership, opportunity, and innovation: what has become concentrated, and how might participation be widened again?

Evidence matters throughout the work. Historical texts, policy documents, public records, and institutional examples are used to support interpretation. They do not replace the argument; they help illuminate it.

The work is provisional because the newest question is still unfolding. Tamil Nadu’s innovation and ownership ecosystems cannot yet be judged with the confidence we bring to earlier struggles over dignity, representation, and educational access.

> The answer changes. The responsibility does not.
